Inflation workshop — first-year economics

Overview

A 60-minute workshop introducing inflation, price level changes, and simple measures used in introductory economics.

Learning purpose

By the end of this session you will be able to:

  • explain what inflation means in everyday and economic terms
  • compare CPI and GDP deflator at an introductory level
  • interpret a simple inflation scenario for different households

Learning Activities

Inflation concepts warm-up

Duration: 15 minsGrouping: Small group

Task: Activate prior knowledge and agree a working definition of inflation.

What to do

Discuss in your group:

  • What changed in the shop prices?
  • Who is most affected?

Task cards

Card 1 — Define inflation

Write a one-sentence definition of inflation in your own words.

Card 2 — Spot the price change

List three prices in the scenario that increased and one that stayed the same.

Scenario A — Grocery prices

Bread rises from £1.20 to £1.50 over a year while wages are unchanged.

Scenario B — Rent and energy

Rent is fixed but energy bills increase sharply for a student household.

Output

A shared definition plus one example from each scenario.

Measures and impacts table

Duration: 20 minsGrouping: Small group

What to do

  • Complete the table and answer the prompt set.

Prompt set

Discussion prompts
  • Which measure would you report to a student newspaper?
  • Who might be harmed most by the scenarios?
  • What policy response is hinted at in the overview?
