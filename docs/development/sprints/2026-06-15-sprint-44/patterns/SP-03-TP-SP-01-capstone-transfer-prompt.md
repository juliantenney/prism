| Field | Value |

---

## Intent

Require the learner to **apply session ideas in a context they select or own**, with **explicit production requirements** (word band and/or elements to include), **multiple application cues**, and **linkage to criteria or key ideas** from the session arc.

---

## Instructional Problem

Transfer materials should move the learner from worked scenarios to production in their own or self-selected context. Evaluation evidence shows the sharpest discrimination in Sprint 44 on this material type:

- **Marx M16** — unanimous **Strong** (Pass 2, Inter-Rater Validation): learner selects a digital platform; word band stated; four application bullets; session criteria named; capstone placement on A4.
- **Photosynthesis M14** — unanimous **Failed** (Pass 2, Inter-Rater): two-sentence body; correct a supplied third-person claim; no operational completion criterion; no own-context framing.
- **Photosynthesis M22** — **verdict split** (Pass 2 Minimum; Inter-Rater Failed): completion band present (`80 words`, `≥2 impacts`) but generic third-person global scenario; no possessive own-context references.

The instructional problem SP-03 solves:

- **FM-02** — transfer body too thin to guide production; no operational completion criterion.
- **FM-03** — third-person procedural transfer without own-context or learner-context selection.

Pass 2 described Marx M16 vs Photosynthesis M14 as the **clearest single-type discrimination** in the evaluation programme.

---

## Minimum Realisation Shape

44-2 §5.8 states minimum requires **both** own-context framing **and** explicit completion criterion. The benchmark set does not contain a material that met minimum with **unanimous** agreement.

**Features evidenced as necessary for minimum (from contract + Failed contrast M14):**

1. **Substantive transfer body** — enough structure that the learner can identify what to produce (M14 Failed because body "too thin to guide production beyond a single sentence task"; Pass 2, Inter-Rater).
2. **Explicit completion criterion** — operational word band or element count (M14 lacked this; M22: `Write at least 80 words and include at least two different impacts`).
3. **Transfer-designated or capstone placement** — M16 on A4 capstone; M22 on A6 capstone (both transfer-flagged in DLA).
4. **At least one substantive transfer prompt** on the activity (all three materials satisfy slot presence).

**Features evidenced on M22 (Pass 2 Minimum only):**

5. **Multi-element application structure** — prompt names multiple impacts or dimensions to address (M22: food systems, climate systems, biodiversity).
6. **New situation framing** — `Apply your reasoning to a new situation` — application beyond the immediate scenario set, though not own-context.

**Not evidenced as sufficient for minimum without dispute:**

- Completion criterion **alone** when own-context is absent — Inter-Rater scored M22 **Failed** applying §5.8 minimum conjunctively (own-context **and** completion both required). Pass 2 scored M22 **Minimum**, treating completion band as threshold met despite third-person framing.

**Authoring implication from evidence:** Treat M14 as the clear Failed floor. Treat M22 as a **documented boundary** — do not treat Pass 2 Minimum as settled precedent for own-context waiver.

---

## Strong Realisation Shape

Abstract strong structure evidenced by Marx M16 (Strong in Pass 2 and Inter-Rater Validation):

1. **Learner-context selection** — `Consider a modern digital platform (e.g. ride-sharing, food delivery, or online marketplaces)`. Evaluation accepted this as own-context: learner chooses and applies to a platform they select (Inter-Rater: "learner selects and applies to a chosen platform").
2. **Operational word band** — `Write a short response (100–150 words) addressing:`.
3. **Multiple application cues** — four bullet prompts requiring distinct analytical moves:
   - apply a core concept (surplus value) to the chosen context;
   - classify actors (bourgeoisie / proletariat);
   - test framework explanatory power;
   - select and justify which session criterion is most useful.
4. **Session criteria linkage** — explicit reference to `evaluation criteria (explanatory power, predictive accuracy, relevance)` named in adjacent Marx M12.
5. **Framework bridge** — closing line ties to session method: `apply the same analytical framework but in a new context`.
6. **Capstone placement** — A4 final evaluation activity with `purpose: transfer` in DLA.
7. **No pre-written learner response** — prompt assigns production; body does not supply completed transfer text.

**Why this is stronger than M22 (Pass 2 Minimum) and M14 (Failed):**

| Feature | M16 Strong | M22 (boundary) | M14 Failed |
| ------- | ---------- | -------------- | ---------- |
| Word/element band | 100–150 words | 80 words, ≥2 impacts | None operational |
| Context framing | Learner selects platform | Generic global scenario | Supplied claim to correct |
| Application cues | Four bullets | One compound prompt | Single correction task |
| Criteria linkage | Named session criteria | None in body | None |
| Body substance | Full section with heading | Three lines | Two sentences |

**Not evidenced as required for Strong in this pattern entry:**

- Limitation cue (`what assumption might fail elsewhere?`) — §5.8 strong signal; absent from M16; M16 still scored Strong unanimously.
- Near/far transfer distinction — not cited in any evaluation verdict for these materials.
- Explicit possessive `your` — M16 uses `Consider` + learner selection rather than `your setting`; evaluators accepted this as own-context.

## Failure Modes Avoided

| FM ID | Name | How SP-03 avoids it |
| ----- | ---- | ------------------- |
| **FM-02** | Thin transfer body | Requires substantive body with operational completion band and multi-cue structure (M16); rejects two-sentence prompts with no word/element count (M14) |
| **FM-03** | Third-person procedural transfer | Requires learner-context selection or own-context framing (M16 platform choice); rejects correct-this-claim-only and generic global scenario without learner context (M14 Failed; M22 flagged in Inter-Rater) |

## Detection Signals

### Strong

Reviewer can confirm (M16 precedent):

- [ ] Transfer on capstone or transfer-designated activity.
- [ ] Operational completion criterion — stated word band and/or required elements.
- [ ] Learner-context selection or possessive own-context framing (`Consider a…`, `your`, `you` applying to chosen setting).
- [ ] Multiple application bullets or cues — not a single correction sentence.
- [ ] Link to session criteria or key ideas named in the arc.
- [ ] No pre-written learner transfer response in the material body.
- [ ] Reviewer can state what the learner must produce and in which context.

### Minimum

Reviewer can confirm threshold features **without** full Strong set:

- [ ] Substantive body (more than one or two bare sentences).
- [ ] Explicit completion band or element count.
- [ ] Capstone / transfer placement.
- [ ] **Boundary:** If own-context absent, record FM-03 risk — Pass 2 may score Minimum (M22); Inter-Rater may score Failed under strict §5.8 conjunctive minimum.

### Weak

Below Strong; may still be Minimum on lenient reading:

- Completion band present but third-person global scenario only (M22 pattern).
- Single compound prompt without multiple application cues.
- No criteria linkage to session arc.
- Thin framing without context selection (approaches M14).

### Failed

Reviewer can confirm (M14 precedent):

- [ ] Body too thin to guide production (`transfer body so thin` — §5.8).
- [ ] No operational completion criterion (`short` without word count).
- [ ] Third-person claim correction only; no own-context or context selection.
- [ ] Reviewer cannot identify completion requirements or production context.

### Boundary Case

| Field | Value |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Material** | Photosynthesis M22 (Activity A6 — capstone) |
| **Corpus** | `benchmark-corpus/photosynthesis/learning-materials.txt` |
| **Verdict** | Pass 2: **Minimum** — Inter-Rater Validation: **Failed** |

**Why evaluators differed:**

| Evaluator | Verdict | Reasoning |
| --------- | ------- | ----------- |
| **Pass 2** | Minimum | "Completion band (80 words, ≥2 impacts); new systemic situation; capstone placement. Strong not met: no limitation cue; no link back to session criteria; hypothetical third-person framing without possessive own-context references. Functional but thin vs Marx M16." |
| **Inter-Rater** | Failed | "Completion criterion present, but generic third-person scenario with no learner-context selection. §5.8 minimum requires **both** own-context and completion criterion — applied conjunctively → Failed." |

**Interpretation issue (documented, not resolved):**

1. **Own-context via learner selection vs possessive reference** — M16 passed own-context without `your`/`your setting` by treating platform **selection** as learner-owned context. M22 supplies a fixed global scenario with no selection step; Pass 2 still awarded Minimum for completion band; Inter-Rater withheld minimum for missing own-context.

2. **Conjunctive vs compensatory minimum** — 44-2 §5.8 lists own-context **and** completion as separate minimum bullets. Inter-Rater read these as conjunctive (both required). Pass 2 treated a strong completion band as sufficient for Minimum when own-context was weak.

3. **Capstone transfer vs misconception-correction transfer** — M14 and M22 serve different activity shapes; M14 Failed unanimously. M22 disagreement is specifically about whether generic new-situation application satisfies own-context when completion criteria are explicit.

**Corpus evidence:**

```text
Apply your reasoning to a new situation:
Explain how a major reduction in photosynthesis might affect human food systems, climate systems, and biodiversity.
Write at least 80 words and include at least two different impacts.
```

## Authoring Guidance

Evidence-backed guidance only.

1. **State an operational completion criterion** — word band and/or required elements. M16: `100–150 words`; M22: `80 words`, `≥2 impacts`. M14 Failed without this (`short` is not operational).

2. **Frame learner-context selection** — invite the learner to choose a setting, role, platform, or case to apply ideas (`Consider a modern digital platform…`). This was the evidenced own-context move on M16. Do not rely on a bare third-person claim to correct (M14 Failed).

3. **Supply multiple application cues** — separate bullets for distinct analytical moves (M16: four bullets). A single correction or single compound sentence is insufficient for Strong and risks Failed when thin (M14).

4. **Link to session criteria or key ideas by name** — M16 references `explanatory power, predictive accuracy, relevance` from the evaluate arc. M22 lacked this; cited as reason Strong was not met in Pass 2.

5. **Close with a framework bridge** — M16: `apply the same analytical framework but in a new context`. Connects transfer to the method practised in the session.

6. **Place on capstone or transfer-designated activity** — all three benchmark transfers were on transfer-flagged activities (A4 Marx, A4/A6 Photosynthesis).

7. **Do not pre-write the learner's transfer response** — no benchmark violated this; remains §5.8 guard.

8. **Avoid two-sentence transfer bodies** — M14 body length correlated with Failed in all passes.

9. **Treat M22 as a cautionary boundary** — completion band alone did not secure agreement across evaluators when own-context was absent. Authors targeting Strong should follow M16, not minimum Pass 2 reading of M22.

---

## Reviewer Guidance

Evaluate a `transfer_prompt` body against 44-2 §5.8 using this sequence:

1. **Slot and placement** — Confirm material on capstone or transfer-designated activity (`purpose: transfer` in DLA).
2. **Failed check** — If body is pointer-thin (M14 scale), no operational completion criterion, or third-person correction only → **Failed** (FM-02, FM-03).
3. **Minimum check** — Apply §5.8 minimum bullets:
   - Own-context or learner-context selection present?
   - Explicit completion criterion present?
   - **Document if applying conjunctively (Inter-Rater) or if completion compensates (Pass 2 on M22).**
4. **Strong check** — Look for M16 signals: word band, multiple application cues, criteria linkage, framework bridge, no pre-written response.
5. **Record failure modes** — FM-02, FM-03 as appropriate.
6. **Boundary flag** — If material resembles M22 (completion band + generic third-person scenario), note verdict-risk in review record; do not treat as settled precedent.

Do not score page-composition fidelity in the same pass (M22 absent from Photosynthesis `page.json` A6 noted in Pass 2 — channel issue only).

## Boundary Notes

**Unanimous agreement:**

| Material | Verdict | Passes |
| -------- | ------- | ------ |
| Marx M16 | Strong | Pass 2, Inter-Rater |
| Photosynthesis M14 | Failed | Pass 2, Inter-Rater |

**Documented disagreement:**

| Material | Pass 2 | Inter-Rater | Issue |
| -------- | ------ | ----------- | ----- |
| Photosynthesis M22 | Minimum | Failed | Own-context conjunctive minimum vs completion-band sufficiency |

**Own-context interpretation issue:**

- M16 satisfied own-context through **learner selection** of a platform type without possessive `your setting` language. Both evaluators scoring M16 Strong accepted this.
- M22 used **generic global impacts** with no selection step. Inter-Rater: does not meet own-context. Pass 2: Minimum on other grounds.
- SP-03 does **not** resolve this by inventing a new rule. Authors aiming for unambiguous Strong should follow M16 (selection + word band + criteria linkage). Reviewers should declare which minimum interpretation they apply when scoring M22-like bodies.

**Strong signals not required in benchmark Strong verdict:**

- Limitation cue (`what assumption might fail elsewhere?`) — absent from M16; not cited as blocking Strong.
- Possessive second-person — absent from M16; not cited as blocking Strong.
