## The Core Idea of Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy. This chemical energy is stored in the molecule glucose, which can later be used to fuel cellular processes.

The overall process can be summarised by this equation:

$$
6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{light energy} \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2
$$

### What each part means

* **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)**: comes from the atmosphere and provides carbon atoms
* **Water (H₂O)**: absorbed through roots and provides hydrogen and electrons
* **Light energy**: captured by chlorophyll and drives the process
* **Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)**: stores chemical energy in bonds
* **Oxygen (O₂)**: released as a by-product

### Key insight

Photosynthesis is both:

* an **energy transformation** (light → chemical energy)
* a **matter transformation** (CO₂ and H₂O → glucose and O₂)

It does not create matter or energy — it rearranges and converts what already exists.

## Worked Example: Interpreting the Equation

### Step 1: Start with inputs

We begin with carbon dioxide and water:

* 6 CO₂ molecules
* 6 H₂O molecules

These supply carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms.

### Step 2: Add energy

Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll, exciting electrons and making reactions possible.

### Step 3: Rearranging matter

Atoms are not created or destroyed:

* Carbon atoms from CO₂ become part of glucose
* Hydrogen atoms from water combine in glucose
* Oxygen atoms are released as O₂

### Step 4: Energy storage

Light energy is converted into chemical energy stored in glucose bonds.

### Conclusion

The process transforms:

* energy form (light → chemical)
* matter arrangement (simple molecules → complex organic molecule)

## The Two Stages of Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis occurs in two linked stages:

### 1. Light-dependent reactions

* Occur in thylakoid membranes
* Require light
* Produce ATP and NADPH
* Release oxygen

### 2. Calvin cycle

* Occurs in the stroma
* Uses ATP and NADPH
* Fixes CO₂ into glucose

### Key link

The first stage captures and stores energy. The second stage uses that energy to build organic molecules.

### Matter pathway

## Photosynthesis and Ecosystems

Photosynthesis forms the base of most ecosystems. Organisms that perform photosynthesis (producers) convert light energy into chemical energy stored in biomass. This energy is then transferred through food chains. Without photosynthesis, ecosystems would lack a primary energy source.
