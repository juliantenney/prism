# Problem Statement

PRISM may be treating content volume as a proxy for instructional value.

The current concern may not be "Can Copilot output enough?" but:

"Can PRISM design pages whose embedded materials are sufficient, bounded, and realistic for the intended learner-time budget?"

## Why this matters

- Overly large pages can reduce activity completion and overwhelm learners.
- Overly thin pages can fail instructional sufficiency.
- Instructional quality depends on task completion feasibility, not raw word count.

## Working hypothesis

Instructionally valid pages may require less embedded content than previously assumed because learner time includes comprehension, planning, production, discussion, analysis, and review — not only reading.
