## Intent

Give the learner **criteria-linked self-check items** before continuing or submitting — testing reasoning and output quality against explicit checkpoints, with **revision guidance** when checks fail — and, for strong realisation, **weak-pattern exposure** and **judgement-oriented retrieval** beyond bare completeness.

---

## Instructional Problem

Checklists should verify learner work against `expected_output` and activity criteria, not merely confirm completion. Sprint 44 evaluation found:

- **Seven surviving checklist bodies** (four Marx, three Photosynthesis) all met §5.7 **minimum** structural threshold in Inter-Rater Validation — ≥4 items, criteria-linked wording, checkable form.
- **FM-11** (checklist at minimum threshold only) was recorded on **all seven** in Inter-Rater Validation and on **all three Photosynthesis** checklists in Pass 2.
- **Pass 2** scored **all four Marx checklists Strong**; **Inter-Rater** scored **the same four Minimum** (FM-11).
- **Photosynthesis M13** **Failed** unanimously — checklist slot with **no checkable body** (`Material: M13 (checklist) — as above`).
- **Photosynthesis M10** attracted **FM-09** (verification completeness bias) in Pass 2 — `Is each row complete?` alongside thin presentation.

The instructional problem SP-05 addresses is **verification that stops at threshold**: checklists that tick §5.7 minimum (item count, criteria tie-in) without reliably elevating retrieval to reasoning quality, weak-pattern exposure, or rubric-aligned judgement — **FM-11**, recurrent **cross-domain**. The pattern also documents **capture failure** when verification slots emit pointer stubs (M13) rather than instructional bodies.

---

## Minimum Realisation Shape

Minimum structure evidenced by **all surviving checklist bodies** in Inter-Rater Validation (Marx M4, M8, M11, M15; Photosynthesis M4, M7, M10):

1. **At least four checkable items** — each benchmark body lists exactly four bullets (or four equivalent list items on M10).
2. **Items tied to activity criteria or expected_output** — not generic completion alone:
   - Marx M4: terms, definitions, scenario application, relationships.
   - Marx M8: mechanism description, mechanism–outcome links, cause→effect, logical consistency.
   - Marx M11: case evidence, alignment judgement, justification, direct comparison.
   - Marx M15: overall judgement, balanced evidence, criteria use, argument structure.
   - Photosynthesis M4: inputs/outputs, energy, matter, terminology.
   - Photosynthesis M7: ATP/NADPH staging, glucose stage, stage links, precision.
   - Photosynthesis M10: energy/matter distinction, explanation correctness, examples, row completeness.
3. **Learner-check wording** — `Have you…`, `Did you…`, `Does your…` (imperative check verbs; not open reflective prompts).
4. **Checkable form** — list items suitable for self-tick.

**Minimum revise support (evidenced variably):**

5. **Revision guidance present on Marx bodies** — M4, M8, M11, M15 each include a `### If…` / revise block directing learner action.
6. **Photosynthesis M4, M7** — revise guidance present (`If any answer is NO`; `If not, revisit…`).
7. **Photosynthesis M10** — **no structured revise section** (Pass 2: thin presentation).

**Minimum does not require** (absent or disputed on benchmark bodies):

- Grouped sections aligned to output structure — **not evidenced** on any benchmark checklist; Inter-Rater cited absence as FM-11 signal on Marx bodies.
- Misconception guard on one item — **not evidenced** on any benchmark checklist in evaluation passes.
- Weak-pattern exposure — **disputed**: Pass 2 credited Marx M4/M8/M11/M15; Inter-Rater scored same bodies Minimum (FM-11) without treating weak-pattern items as Strong.

## Strong Realisation Shape

Strong realisation is **the most calibration-sensitive dimension** in the Sprint 44 pattern set. Features below are tagged by evidence source.

### Evidenced on Marx checklists — Pass 2 Strong verdict only

1. **Items expose weak reasoning patterns** (§5.7 strong signal) — Pass 2 cited explicitly:
   - M4: `not just named it`; `not described them in general`.
   - M11: `evidence from the cases, not general statements`.
   - M8/M15: reasoning and balance checks framed as quality tests, not completion.
2. **Explicit revise path** — all four Marx bodies include heading-level revise guidance with specific remediation (M4: correct definitions, add links; M8: revisit table; M11: add evidence; M15: improve balance).
3. **Judgement-oriented verification** (evaluate arcs) — M11: alignment judgement + justification; M15: clear overall judgement, evidence on both sides, `at least two criteria explicitly`.
4. **Criteria / rubric linkage on capstone** — M15 items map to evaluative `expected_output` and criteria named in adjacent M12 (Pass 2: "maps to rubric/criteria dimensions").

### Evidenced as absent or weak on Photosynthesis Minimum bodies

5. **Reasoning-quality dominance over completeness** — Pass 2: Photosynthesis M4 items "completeness-oriented rather than evaluative depth"; M10 `Is each row complete?` → **FM-09**.
6. **Structured verification framing** — Photosynthesis M4/M7 have section headings; M10 is bare bullets without heading or revise block (Pass 2).

### Not evidenced as present on any benchmark checklist

7. **Grouped sections** (evidence / reasoning / judgement / presentation) — Inter-Rater listed absence on Marx bodies; no body demonstrated grouped sections.
8. **Misconception guard** — §5.7 optional strong signal; **no benchmark checklist** received evaluation credit for this feature.

### Suggested strong shape (Pass 2 Marx features + §5.7 contract; Inter-Rater disputed)

9. **Weak-pattern guards + judgement retrieval + explicit revise path** — Pass 2 treated Marx M4–M15 as Strong on these grounds; Inter-Rater treated the **same bodies** as FM-11 Minimum. SP-05 does **not** resolve which reading is correct.

**Why Pass 2 rated Marx Strong and Photosynthesis Minimum (both passes agree on Photosynthesis band):**

| Signal | Marx M4 (example) | Photosynthesis M4 (example) |
| ------ | ----------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Weak-pattern exposure | `not just named`; `not in general` | Not cited |
| Revise block | `### If any check is not met` + actions | Present but thinner |
| Item orientation | Application + relationships | Inputs/outputs + terminology completeness |
| Pass 2 | Strong | Minimum |
| Inter-Rater | Minimum (FM-11) | Minimum (FM-11) |

## Detection Signals

### Minimum

Reviewer can confirm (all seven surviving bodies, Inter-Rater):

- [ ] ≥4 checkable items.
- [ ] Items reference task evidence, criteria, or output structure.
- [ ] Learner-check imperative wording.
- [ ] List form suitable for self-tick.
- [ ] **FM-11 likely** if weak-pattern guards, grouped sections, misconception guard, and rubric-dimension mapping are absent — Inter-Rater applied this to **all** Marx and Photosynthesis surviving checklists.

### Strong

Reviewer can confirm — **Pass 2 Marx precedent; Inter-Rater withheld on same bodies**:

- [ ] Items expose weak reasoning patterns (`not just…`, `not in general`, `from the cases not general statements`).
- [ ] Judgement- or evidence-quality retrieval on evaluate arcs (M11, M15).
- [ ] Explicit revise section with task-specific remediation.
- [ ] Capstone items map to named criteria (M15 ↔ M12).
- [ ] **Declare calibration stance** — Pass 2 Strong vs Inter-Rater Minimum on identical Marx prose.

### Weak

Below Strong; may still be Minimum:

- Completeness-oriented items without weak-pattern guards (Photosynthesis M4 — Pass 2).
- Single-line revise cue without structured `### If` block (Photosynthesis M7).
- Bare bullet list without verification heading (Photosynthesis M10).
- Completion-bias item present (M10 row completeness — FM-09 risk).

### Failed

Reviewer can confirm (M13 precedent):

- [ ] Checklist label/slot present with **no checkable items** in GAM body.
- [ ] Pointer-only emission (`as above`) — stub detection rule.
- [ ] Fewer than four items when verification function required.
- [ ] Reviewer cannot map any item to `expected_output`.

## Authoring Guidance

Evidence-backed guidance only.

1. **Provide ≥4 criteria-linked checkable items** — universal minimum on surviving benchmark bodies; M13 Failed without this.

2. **Tie each item to `expected_output` or activity criteria** — not `Did you finish?` (not evidenced on benchmark set but §5.7 guard).

3. **Use learner-check imperatives** — `Have you…`, `Did you…`, `Does your…`.

4. **Include explicit revise guidance** — all Marx bodies and Photosynthesis M4/M7; M10 lacked it and scored weaker. Use structured `### If…` blocks with specific remediation where possible (Marx model).

5. **Expose weak reasoning patterns where task allows** — Pass 2 credited M4 (`not just named`, `not in general`), M11 (`from the cases, not general statements`). Inter-Rater did not treat these alone as clearing FM-11.

6. **Prefer reasoning-quality checks over row/table completion** — avoid completion-dominant items when verifying analysis (M10 `Is each row complete?` → FM-09).

7. **On evaluate/capstone arcs, require judgement and evidence retrieval** — M11 alignment + justification; M15 balanced evidence + explicit criteria use (Pass 2 Strong features).

8. **Do not emit stub or pointer-only bodies** — M13 `as above` → Failed; 44-1 / stub detection scope.

9. **Grouped sections and misconception guards** — §5.7 strong signals; **not evidenced** on any benchmark checklist. May be authoring targets; not SP-05 requirements from Sprint 44 evidence.

10. **Do not append cognition-cue blocks to checklist bodies** — Photosynthesis M4 follows checklist with cognition cues (FM-07 pattern on adjacent materials); keep one move per material.

## Reviewer Guidance

Evaluate a `checklist` body against 44-2 §5.7:

1. **Failed check** — Count items in GAM body literally. Pointer/stub (`as above`) → Failed (M13). &lt;4 items → Failed.
2. **Minimum check** — ≥4 items; criteria-linked; learner-check wording; checkable form (all seven surviving bodies).
3. **FM-11 check** — Record if grouped sections, misconception guard, rubric mapping, and weak-pattern exposure are absent. Inter-Rater applied to **all** surviving bodies including Marx.
4. **FM-09 check** — Flag completion-dominant items (M10 pattern).
5. **Strong check** — Apply Pass 2 Marx criteria (weak-pattern exposure, judgement retrieval, revise path, criteria linkage) **or** Inter-Rater FM-11 threshold. **Declare which stance** in review record.
6. **DLA cross-check** — M13 showed obligation gap; stub vs instructional quality are separate channels.
7. **Composition** — Separate page-composition loss from body score.

SP-05 does **not** resolve Marx Strong/Minimum split.
