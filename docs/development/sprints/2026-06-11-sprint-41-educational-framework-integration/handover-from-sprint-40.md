## Key Discoveries

### Learner Journey

The learner journey is the primary design unit.

PRISM should generate coherent learner-development journeys rather than collections of content pages or disconnected activities.

### Learner Transformations

Educational quality is defined by the development of:

- Understanding
- Capability
- Judgement
- Independence

### Judgement

Judgement is a central learner transformation.

PRISM outputs should create opportunities for learners to compare, evaluate, justify, critique and defend decisions.

### Metacognition

Metacognition is a cross-cutting process.

It supports learners in recognising understanding, uncertainty, progress, confidence, decisions and next steps.

### Learning Success

Learning success is an indicator of meaningful development.

PRISM should make learner progress visible so learners can recognise what they now understand, can do, can judge and can manage independently.

### Progressive Independence

Learning journeys should progressively reduce support and transfer responsibility to the learner.

A typical progression is:

Observe
→ Guided Practice
→ Supported Decision Making
→ Independent Decision Making
→ Transfer

### Cognitive Activity over Interface Activity

PRISM should optimise for cognitive activity rather than interface activity.

A learner thinking deeply with a page and pencil may be doing richer learning than a learner completing low-value on-screen interactions.
