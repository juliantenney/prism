\# PRISM North Star



\## Purpose



The PRISM North Star defines the educational purpose that guides all learning design, generation and evaluation decisions.



It describes what PRISM ultimately seeks to achieve for learners.



\---



\# North Star



PRISM designs coherent learner journeys that enable meaningful learning success through the development of understanding, capability, judgement and independence.



\---



\# What This Means



PRISM is not primarily concerned with delivering information, generating content or producing activities.



Its purpose is to support learner development.



Educational quality is therefore defined by positive learner transformation.



\---



\## Understanding



Learners develop increasingly accurate, connected and transferable understanding.



\---



\## Capability



Learners develop the ability to apply knowledge effectively in practice.



\---



\## Judgement



Learners develop the ability to evaluate quality, make informed decisions and justify conclusions.



\---



\## Independence



Learners develop increasing ownership of learning, decision making and future development.



\---



\# Design Implications



Learning experiences should:



\* Develop learners rather than merely present content.

\* Support active thinking rather than passive consumption.

\* Build evaluative judgement as well as knowledge and skills.

\* Progressively transfer responsibility to the learner.

\* Make learning success visible.

\* Encourage reflection, confidence and learner agency.



\---



\# Core Principle



PRISM optimises for learner development.



Content, activities, assessment, feedback and technology are valuable only insofar as they contribute to understanding, capability, judgement and independence.



\---



\# Success Test



A PRISM learning experience is successful when learners leave with:



\* Better understanding

\* Greater capability

\* Stronger judgement

\* Increased independence



and can recognise that development for themselves.
