Linking Experience to Theory

Duration: 25 mins

Study orientation:

This self-study page moves from Marx's life phases to comparing texts and applying concepts. Allow about 60 minutes total and keep brief notes as you work.

Intellectual frame: You are thinking like a historian connecting biography, texts, and social change.

Before analysing Marx's ideas, first consider how political exile might shape someone's thinking. This activity helps connect historical context to Marx's critique of capitalism.

Before you start: Recall one example of how living conditions might influence a thinker's priorities.

Task: Connect biographical experience to theoretical development.

Comparing Marx's Major Works

Duration: 20 mins

Connection to previous activity: Build on the cause–effect links you traced in the previous activity when judging each text's audience and purpose.

As you compare these texts, notice how Marx's political and economic arguments differ.

Disciplinary lens: Thinking as a historian comparing texts

How to think: Focus on purpose, audience, and argument style—not only topic overlap.

Using evidence: Cite a phrase from each excerpt when you fill the comparison table.

Structuring your response: Claim → evidence from the text → implication for the reader.

Key distinction: Contrast revolutionary programme with systematic critique — do not treat the works as identical calls to action.
