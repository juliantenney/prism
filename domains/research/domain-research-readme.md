# domain-research-readme.md

## Purpose

The Research Domain Pack provides guidance for generating workflows that support research-oriented tasks such as analysis, synthesis, comparison, and evidence-based reasoning.

It complements the General Domain Pack by introducing domain-specific expectations around:
- fidelity to source material
- traceability of claims
- structured analysis
- preservation of uncertainty
- evidence-based synthesis

---

## How it fits within PRISM

PRISM workflow generation uses layered context:

General + Domain + Task

When the Research domain is selected:

- General Domain Pack → ensures structural quality and consistency
- Research Domain Pack → ensures research rigour and analytical integrity

Research workflows also support structured sequential execution with step-level runner guidance, while preserving system metadata separately from runner-visible instructions.

---

## When to use the Research Domain Pack

Use this domain when the goal involves:

- analysing academic or technical texts
- conducting literature reviews
- extracting findings or evidence
- comparing sources
- identifying themes or arguments
- generating research questions
- producing summaries, briefings, or annotated notes

---

## What this domain influences

The Research pack shapes:

### 1. Workflow structure
Encourages inclusion of steps such as:
- Normalize Content
- Extract Key Findings
- Model Argument Structure
- Build Evidence Map
- Thematic Analysis
- Validation

### 2. Artefacts
Promotes use of structured, reusable artefacts:
- normalized_content
- key_findings
- argument_structure
- evidence_map
- thematic_analysis
- literature_matrix
- research_summary

### 3. Prompt design
Ensures prompts:
- remain grounded in source content
- distinguish extraction vs interpretation vs synthesis
- preserve uncertainty and limitations
- enforce structured outputs

---

## Relationship to other domains

### With General
- General ensures good workflow design
- Research ensures methodological quality and rigour

### With Pedagogy
- Pedagogy focuses on learning and teaching
- Research focuses on analysis and evidence

These domains can be combined where appropriate, e.g.:
- General + Research → research workflows
- General + Pedagogy → learning design workflows
- General + Research + Pedagogy → research-informed learning materials

---

## Design philosophy

The Research domain assumes:

- not all inputs are equally reliable
- not all claims are equally supported
- ambiguity and uncertainty are important
- structure improves clarity and reuse

It prioritises:

- accuracy over fluency
- traceability over simplicity
- clarity over compression

---

## Summary

The Research Domain Pack enables PRISM to:

- produce analytically rigorous workflows
- preserve evidence and nuance
- generate reusable research artefacts
- support a wide range of research tasks

It should be used whenever the primary task involves understanding, analysing, or synthesising information rather than simply transforming it.
