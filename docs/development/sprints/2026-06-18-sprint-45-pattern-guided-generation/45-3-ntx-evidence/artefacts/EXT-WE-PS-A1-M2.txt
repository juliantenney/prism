Worked example: unpacking the photosynthesis equation

Equation:
6 CO2 + 6 H2O + light energy -> C6H12O6 + 6 O2

Step 1 - Identify inputs and outputs
Inputs are carbon dioxide, water, and light energy. Outputs are glucose and oxygen.

Step 2 - Explain what happens to matter
Atoms are rearranged, not created or destroyed. Carbon atoms from CO2 and hydrogen/oxygen atoms from H2O are reorganized into glucose and oxygen molecules.

Step 3 - Explain what happens to energy
Light energy absorbed by chlorophyll is converted into chemical energy carriers, then stored in the bonds of glucose.

Step 4 - State the biological significance
Glucose provides stored chemical energy and building material for growth. Oxygen is released as a by-product.

Conclusion:
Photosynthesis transforms energy form while conserving matter, linking sunlight to biomass production in ecosystems.
