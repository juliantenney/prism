Activity: Classify Key Inflation Concepts  
Activity ID: A1  
Mapped outcomes: Understand the concept of inflation and how it affects the overall economy.

Material: M1 (classification_table)  
Purpose: Provide terms and definitions related to inflation for classification  
Content:  
| Term           | Definition                                                                                   | Example                                      | Category (to classify)       |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Inflation      | A general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money over time.            | Prices of groceries rising year over year.   |                              |
| Deflation      | A general decrease in prices and increase in the purchasing power of money.                  | Falling prices during a recession.            |                              |
| Hyperinflation | Extremely rapid or out-of-control inflation, often exceeding 50% per month.                   | Zimbabwe’s inflation in the late 2000s.      |                              |
| Stagflation    | A situation where inflation is high but economic growth is stagnant and unemployment is high.| 1970s US economy with rising prices and unemployment.|                              |

Activity: Compare Inflation Measurement Methods  
Activity ID: A2  
Mapped outcomes: Explain the Consumer Price Index (CPI) and its role in measuring inflation.

Material: M2 (comparison_table)  
Purpose: Provide data and descriptions of CPI and alternative inflation measurement methods  
Content:  
| Measure               | Definition                                                                                     | Calculation Method                                  | Typical Uses                                  |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Consumer Price Index (CPI)  | Measures average change over time in prices paid by consumers for a market basket of goods and services. | Tracks prices of a fixed basket of consumer goods and services monthly or quarterly. | Used to track cost of living changes and inflation targeting by governments. |
| Producer Price Index (PPI)  | Measures average change over time in selling prices received by domestic producers for their output. | Tracks prices at the wholesale or producer level before goods reach consumers. | Used to predict future consumer inflation and analyze inflation at production stage. |
| GDP Deflator           | Measures price changes for all goods and services included in GDP, reflecting inflation across the economy. | Calculated as (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) × 100.      | Used to measure overall inflation in the economy including investment and government spending. |

Activity: Evaluate Strategies to Manage Inflation Effects  
Activity ID: A4  
Mapped outcomes: Evaluate strategies households can use to manage the effects of inflation on their finances.

Material: M4 (impact_table)  
Purpose: List common household financial strategies and their effectiveness against inflation  
Content:  
| Strategy                   | Description                                                                                      | Pros                                                      | Cons                                                      | Effectiveness Rating (1=Low, 5=High) |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Budgeting Adjustments      | Revising household budgets to prioritize essential expenses and reduce discretionary spending. | Helps control spending, immediate impact on finances.     | May reduce quality of life, requires discipline.          | 4                                   |
| Investment Diversification | Spreading investments across assets like stocks, bonds, and real estate to hedge against inflation. | Potential to grow wealth and offset inflation losses.     | Risk of market volatility, requires financial knowledge.  | 5                                   |
| Price Comparison Shopping  | Actively comparing prices and seeking discounts to reduce costs on goods and services.          | Saves money on everyday purchases, easy to implement.     | Time-consuming, savings may be limited.                    | 3                                   |
| Fixed-Rate Borrowing       | Taking loans with fixed interest rates to avoid rising borrowing costs during inflation.         | Protects against rising interest rates, predictable costs.| Not always accessible, increases debt burden.             | 4                                   |
