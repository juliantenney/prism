## Intent

Show an expert completing **one instance step-by-step** with **labelled steps, visible reasoning between steps, and a completed model walkthrough** — and, for strong realisation, an **explicit bridge** telling the learner how to reuse the same method on their parallel independent task.

---

## Instructional Problem

A `worked_example` must make expert reasoning visible before the learner attempts a similar but non-identical task. Sprint 44 sentinel evaluation (Pass 1) and inter-rater validation found that **both benchmark domains produced workable worked examples** — neither M2 body Failed — but **both repeatedly stopped short of the strong signal that connects modelling to the learner's upcoming task**.

Cross-domain observation:

- **Marx M2** and **Photosynthesis M2** each supply four ordered steps, intermediate results, and a conclusion.
- **All evaluation passes** scored Photosynthesis M2 **Minimum**.
- **Pass 1** scored Marx M2 **Strong**; **Inter-Rater Validation** scored Marx M2 **Minimum**, citing the same weakness as Photosynthesis M2.
- **FM-05** (worked example without parallel-task bridge) was recorded on **both materials** in Inter-Rater Validation and on Photosynthesis M2 in Pass 1.

The instructional problem SP-06 addresses is not "no worked example" but **incomplete method transfer**: the learner can follow the expert chain on the model item yet is not told how to apply the same method on their assigned deliverable. Evaluation treated this as the **recurrent cross-domain weakness** on `worked_example`, while step structure and reasoning visibility separated Marx M2 (interpretive depth) from Photosynthesis M2 (inventory-style steps) within the Minimum band.

---

## Minimum Realisation Shape

Minimum structure evidenced by **both** Marx M2 and Photosynthesis M2 (all passes scored at least Minimum):

1. **At least three ordered steps or stages with intermediate results** — both exemplars use four labelled steps (`Step 1` … `Step 4`) plus a conclusion.
2. **Visible reasoning between steps** — not answer-only. Marx M2: because-style moves (`The worker is exploited because…`). Photosynthesis M2: brief causal commentary (`exciting electrons and making reactions possible`; conservation of atoms). Pass 1 noted Photosynthesis reasoning is often inventory/description; Inter-Rater still awarded Minimum.
3. **A clear completed instance or model walkthrough** — Marx M2 completes analysis of the factory scenario; Photosynthesis M2 completes equation interpretation through energy and matter transformation.
4. **Worked item distinct from learner's independent deliverable** — Marx A1 DLA: learner applies structure to a *new* scenario; worked example uses factory worker (£200/£80), not the learner's assigned item. Photosynthesis A1: learner writes own explanation of photosynthesis; worked example walks the equation without supplying the learner's 100–150 word deliverable verbatim.
5. **Step labels or headings a learner can follow** — `Step 1: Identify the classes`, `Step 1: Start with inputs`, etc.

**Minimum does not require** (not evidenced as present on either M2):

- Explicit parallel-task bridge (FM-05 — absent on both).
- Weak/strong evaluative contrast (not applicable to A1 modelling arc).

**Secondary observation on Photosynthesis M2 only (not FM-05):**

- Cognition-cue block appended after the worked-example body (Pass 1, Inter-Rater) — **FM-07** one-move-per-material contamination. Does not negate Minimum on the instructional body but weakens strong realisation.

---

## Strong Realisation Shape

Strong realisation combines **features evidenced on Marx M2 in Pass 1** with **the bridge requirement repeatedly identified as missing** in Inter-Rater Validation and Pass 1 (Photosynthesis).

### Evidenced on Marx M2 (Pass 1 Strong verdict)

1. **Step → meaning pairs** — each step names a move and applies it with conceptual labels (classes, surplus value, exploitation, alienation).
2. **Because/therefore-type reasoning between steps** — e.g. exploitation explained, not only calculated (`exploited because they produce more value than they receive`).
3. **Quantitative or interpretive moves explained** — surplus value arithmetic (£200 − £80) followed by interpretive claim about the capitalist system.
4. **One coherent method** — single analytic sequence from scenario → concepts → conclusion; reviewer can trace one expert decision per step (Pass 1).
5. **Conclusion integrating steps** — closes the chain without pre-writing the learner's independent deliverable.

### Evidenced as absent on both M2 bodies (repeated evaluator observation → FM-05)

6. **Explicit link to the parallel task** — §5.2 strong signal: `"use the same method on…"`. Neither Marx M2 nor Photosynthesis M2 contains this bridge. Inter-Rater scored **both Minimum** primarily on this gap. Pass 1 awarded Marx M2 Strong **despite** the absent bridge, then flagged the bridge as the differentiator between Marx M2 and Photosynthesis M2 within sentinel set.

### Suggested strong shape (contract + evaluation observation, not evidenced as a single Strong body in corpus)

7. **Parallel-task bridge** — a closing or introductory sentence directing the learner to apply the same step sequence on their assigned scenario or explanation task. Evaluation consistently treated this as the move that would elevate Minimum to Strong; no benchmark M2 body demonstrated it.

**Why interpretive depth elevated Marx M2 over Photosynthesis M2 within Minimum/Strong boundary (Pass 1):**

| Signal | Marx M2 | Photosynthesis M2 |
| ------ | ------- | ------------------- |
| Step → meaning | Class roles, exploitation because-claim | Input inventory (`These supply carbon…`) |
| Reasoning type | Interpretive + quantitative | Descriptive / conservation statements |
| Pass 1 verdict | Strong | Minimum |
| Inter-Rater verdict | Minimum (FM-05) | Minimum (FM-05) |

**Not evidenced as required for Strong in this pattern entry:**

- Weak vs strong contrast (evaluate arcs only; not in A1 set).
- Annotated quality features (not cited in M2 evaluations).
- Parallel-task bridge present in corpus (absent — documented as gap, not exemplar).

## Detection Signals

### Minimum

Reviewer can confirm (both M2 precedents):

- [ ] ≥3 labelled or numbered steps with intermediate results.
- [ ] Reasoning visible between steps (not answer-only).
- [ ] Completed walkthrough of the worked item.
- [ ] Worked item topic/data distinct from learner's independent deliverable.
- [ ] Step headings followable by a solo learner.

### Strong

Reviewer can confirm **evidenced Pass 1 signals on Marx M2** plus **bridge** (latter not in corpus):

- [ ] Step → meaning pairs — conceptual move named and applied per step.
- [ ] Because/so/therefore reasoning between steps.
- [ ] Quantitative or interpretive moves explained, not only stated.
- [ ] One coherent method; one traceable expert decision per step.
- [ ] **Explicit parallel-task bridge** — learner told to reuse method on assigned task (§5.2 strong; FM-05 remediation). **Absent on both benchmark M2 bodies** — Inter-Rater withheld Strong for this reason.

### Weak

Below strong; may still be Minimum:

- Steps list inputs/outputs or inventory without sustained interpretive commentary (Pass 1: Photosynthesis M2 pattern).
- Mechanical procedure with thin because/therefore links.
- Cognition-cue or non-modelling content appended to worked-example body (Photosynthesis M2 — FM-07).
- Conclusion restates steps without method-transfer bridge (both M2).

### Failed

Not evidenced on M2 bodies. §5.2 Failed would apply to blank templates, answer keys without reasoning chain, or worked item identical to learner deliverable — none observed in Sprint 44 sentinel set.

### Cross-Domain Observation

**FM-05 appeared on both domains and across evaluators.**

| Material | Domain | FM-05 recorded | Verdict agreement |
| -------- | ------ | -------------- | ----------------- |
| Marx M2 | Marx | Inter-Rater explicit; Pass 1 implicit (Strong despite gap) | Pass 1 Strong / IR Minimum |
| Photosynthesis M2 | Photosynthesis | Pass 1 + Inter-Rater | Both Minimum |

**What evaluators agreed on:**

1. Both bodies meet §5.2 **minimum** structural threshold (steps, reasoning, completed instance, distinct item).
2. Neither body contains an **explicit parallel-task bridge**.
3. The missing bridge is the **recurrent weakness** — Readiness Assessment classified FM-05 as **genuine, cross-domain, recurrent**.

**What evaluators disagreed on:**

1. Whether Marx M2's interpretive depth (step→meaning, because-claims, quantitative interpretation) compensates for missing bridge → **Pass 1 Strong** vs **Inter-Rater Minimum**.
2. Whether Photosynthesis M2's descriptive steps constitute sufficient "visible reasoning" for strong — **both passes: Minimum only**.

**Instructional implication from evidence:**

- Step structure alone does not discriminate Strong across domains reliably when the bridge is absent.
- The parallel-task bridge is the **most stable cross-evaluator differentiator** between minimum threshold and strong realisation on `worked_example`, even though **no benchmark body demonstrates it**.

Pass 1 composition note (channel, not body): Photosynthesis M2 absent from composed `page.json` A1 — learner may not see worked example on page despite GAM body presence. Recorded separately; does not change M2 body verdicts.

## Authoring Guidance

Evidence-backed guidance only.

1. **Provide ≥3 labelled steps with intermediate results** — both M2 exemplars use four steps; minimum threshold met.

2. **Make reasoning visible between steps** — use because/so/therefore interpretive moves, not only inventory (Pass 1 contrast: Marx M2 exploitation step vs Photosynthesis M2 `These supply carbon…`).

3. **Use step → meaning pairs** — name the move and apply it conceptually (Marx M2: identify classes, then interpret surplus value, then explain exploitation).

4. **Complete the worked item without pre-writing the learner's deliverable** — distinct scenario or walkthrough item (factory worker; equation interpretation) separate from learner's independent task.

5. **Add an explicit parallel-task bridge** — direct the learner to apply the same method on their assigned task. **Not present in either benchmark M2**; Inter-Rater and Pass 1 (Photosynthesis) cite absence as FM-05. This is the evidenced elevation move from evaluation, drawn from §5.2 strong realisation text and repeated findings, not from a Strong corpus body.

6. **Explain quantitative or interpretive moves** — arithmetic plus interpretation (Marx M2 Step 2–3) scored higher than inventory listing alone (Photosynthesis M2).

7. **Keep one coherent method per example** — single analytic sequence; both M2 exemplars demonstrate one method.

8. **Do not append cognition-cue blocks to worked-example bodies** — Photosynthesis M2 contamination (FM-07); keep one move per material.

9. **Do not substitute sample_output for worked_example** — Marx A1 lists both M2 and M3; distinct functions per §5.2 / §5.5 relationship in 44-2.

## Reviewer Guidance

Evaluate a `worked_example` body against 44-2 §5.2:

1. **Failed check** — Blank template, answer-only key, or worked item identical to learner deliverable (not evidenced on M2; apply per contract).
2. **Minimum check** — ≥3 steps; visible reasoning; completed instance; distinct item; followable labels (both M2 meet this in all passes).
3. **Strong check — evidenced signals** — Step→meaning; because/therefore; interpretive depth; one method (Pass 1 Marx M2).
4. **Strong check — bridge** — Explicit parallel-task link present? If absent, record **FM-05** (Inter-Rater withheld Strong on both M2 for this reason).
5. **Calibration** — Declare whether interpretive depth can compensate for missing bridge (Pass 1) or bridge is required for Strong (Inter-Rater). SP-06 does not resolve this; document in review record.
6. **Contamination** — Flag cognition-cue appendages separately (FM-07).
7. **Composition** — Record page-level omission separately from body score.
