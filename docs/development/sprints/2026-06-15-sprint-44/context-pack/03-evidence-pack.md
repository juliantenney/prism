## Marx Findings

### Strengths

- **Upstream coherence** — Manual workflow run (“Was Marx Right?”) produced educationally coherent artefacts end-to-end: strong governing inquiry in learning content; capability progression in outcomes; instructional beats in episode plans; operational journey in DLA (core ideas → mechanism → predictions → comparison → judgement).
- **GAM substance** — Activity materials carried real exposition, worked examples, comparison tables, checklists, perspectives, decision frameworks, transfer prompts, and consolidation scaffolds. GAM did not collapse depth on this run.
- **Intellectual movement** — Learning sequence transitions described pedagogical movement (understanding → application → analysis → evaluation), not administrative handoffs.
- **Judgement development** — Evaluation archetype strong in final activities; decision-oriented materials present upstream.
- **Inquiry preservation** — DLA operationalised investigation without destroying upstream inquiry arc.

### Weaknesses

- **Rendered experience activity-led** — Design Page and render produced Overview → Learning Outcomes → Learning Activities → Activity 1…5. Inquiry present but structurally subordinate.
- **Salience failure** — Governing question and investigation arc not the dominant first-impression frame despite upstream strength.
- **PEL buried** — Metacognitive cues preserved on rows but weakly visible; not a sustained page-level spine.
- **Material-weight dominance** — Tables, worked examples, and checklists dominated visual scan path within activities.
- **Prototype v4 gaps** (manual manifestation, not pipeline) — Even strong prototype left room for: interim synthesis; deeper metacognitive voice; tutorial/authorial framing; more explicit judgement criteria.

### Major Educational Conclusions

1. Marx is the **positive case** for upstream generation quality — the workflow can produce a full self-study arc.
2. Marx is the **negative case** for pipeline manifestation — structure exists but does not own learner experience without deliberate manifestation.
3. Marx Resource Prototype (investigation-owned, two-column) scored **8–9/10** in blind review — ownership direction is educationally valid.
4. Marx remains primary benchmark for **44-2 material evaluation** because materials are substantively present to judge against contracts.

## Photosynthesis Findings

### Strengths

- **Architecture generalises** — Same workflow produced a structurally coherent photosynthesis self-study resource without domain-specific architectural redesign.
- **Activity and material scaffolding present** — Tasks, tables, and activity sequence formed a recognisable self-study workbook shape.
- **Cross-domain validation** — Confirms Sprint 43 ownership model and workflow stages are not Marx-specific accidents.
- **Diagnostic clarity** — Weaknesses appear as **material function failure**, not missing pipeline stages — useful contrast domain for depth contracts.

### Weaknesses

- **Exposition thin** — Text and teaching prose often insufficient to connect ideas before practice; reliance on tables or task instructions instead of exposition materials.
- **Modelling weak** — Expert reasoning, criteria walkthroughs, and decision rules less visible than task structure.
- **Worked support uneven** — Modelled reasoning before practice not consistently realised at educational minimum.
- **Closure collapse** — Consolidation and reflection often thin, substituted by prompt sets, or absent as distinct instructional moves.
- **Activity-led feel persists** — Same manifestation pattern as Marx pipeline: structurally correct workbook, weak investigation salience.
- **Table-heavy tendency** — Structured grids present; companion exposition, modelling, and verification moves thinner.

### Major Educational Conclusions

1. Photosynthesis confirms **architecture is not the bottleneck**.
2. Photosynthesis exposes **instructional material realisation** as the recurring cross-domain gap.
3. Photosynthesis is the **secondary evaluation domain** for 44-2 — especially where Marx materials are stronger and may mask contract failure modes.
4. Photosynthesis suggests validation focused only on structure/presence/coverage will **miss** educational failure.

## Salience Findings

### Why Presence and Salience Diverged

**Presence** means a signal exists in an artefact — a field populated, a prompt block applied, a material row listed, a governing question in learning content.

**Salience** means a learner or reviewer encounters that signal as **organising** the resource on a normal read — first impression, section logic, what the resource “is.”

They diverged because:

1. **Compose authority** — Design Page is structurally organised around learning activities and verbatim-preserved materials. Hard contracts favour activity membership and material fidelity over inquiry spine mapping.
2. **No page-level inquiry mapping** — Learning content influences wrapper prose and assimilation but lacks hard section authority at compose.
3. **Material visual weight** — Rich tables and worked content render inside activity blocks, pulling attention to tasks before arc.
4. **PEL row-local** — Metacognitive fields preserved per activity but not elevated to journey-level framing.
5. **Wrapper thinness** — Overview and study tips may carry inquiry language without structural precedence over the activity list.

**Conclusion:** High presence across GLC, DLA, GAM, and fields coexisted with low salience on rendered page. The learner experienced **tasks about a topic** more than **one investigation unfolding**.

**Sprint 44 note:** Salience findings remain true for pipeline render. Marx prototype showed salience can improve through manifestation without denying that **material realisation quality** is now the sharper lever for Sprint 44.

## Instructional Depth Findings

Distilled from Marx, Photosynthesis, workbook gap analyses, and implicit contract reconstruction. These inform 44-2; they are not new contracts.

### Explanation Quality (`text`)

- **Finding:** Exposition often absent, thin, or replaced by table labels and task instructions.
- **Strong case (Marx):** GAM carried usable teaching prose upstream.
- **Weak case (Photosynthesis):** Prose did not reliably link ≥2 ideas before practice.
- **Failure mode:** Specification synopsis masquerading as material body.

### Worked Examples (`worked_example`)

- **Finding:** Modelled reasoning before practice is contractually expected but often missing, or substituted by blank templates / answer keys.
- **Strong case (Marx):** Worked examples with step structure present in GAM.
- **Weak case:** Template-only or single-step demonstration without reasoning chain.
- **Failure mode:** Pre-completing the learner’s parallel deliverable.

### Tutorial Voice / Authorial Framing

- **Finding:** Materials tend toward procedural completion tone rather than guided intellectual apprenticeship (“why this matters now”).
- **Prototype insight:** v4 targets explicitly call for stronger tutorial voice — a manifestation authorial choice that also applies to material prose quality.
- **Failure mode:** Facilitator usage notes or task imperatives without teaching voice.

### Misconception Repair (`misconception_note`)

- **Finding:** Misconceptions appear in knowledge model and support_note patterns; dedicated learner-facing misconception callouts inconsistently realised.
- **Failure mode:** Facilitator-only failure_mode JSON without learner material.
- **Contract density:** Prompt-level; weakest runtime enforcement.

### Transfer (`transfer_prompt`)

- **Finding:** Transfer beats exist upstream; own-context prompts with completion criteria uneven.
- **Strong case (Marx):** Transfer prompts in GAM on capstone activities.
- **Failure mode:** Third-person scenarios only; missing “your context” production requirement.

### Synthesis / Consolidation (`consolidation_summary`)

- **Finding:** Session closure often fails — prompt_set substitutes for consolidation; table reprint instead of synthesis scaffold; spoiler consolidation pre-writes learner closure.
- **Failure mode:** Past-tense “you have learned” essay when learner must write; absent ≥3 closure angles (remember / changed / apply).

### Judgement Criteria (`rubric`, `quality_criteria`)

- **Finding:** Compare/rank tasks often lack operational criteria before learner judgement; pre-filled rating cells undermine evaluative judgement.
- **Strong case (Marx prototype review):** Judgement development scored highest when criteria explicit.
- **Failure mode:** Rank table with pre-supplied effectiveness scores; criteria labels without usable detail.

### Cross-Cutting Depth Patterns

| Pattern | Observation |
| ------- | ----------- |
| Function collapse | One material absorbs exposition + modelling + verification + closure |
| Scaffold ≠ deliverable | Consolidation and sample output pre-answer learner tasks |
| Fading arc breaks | Independent stage repeats modelled answer |
| Presence without move | Material type label present; instructional function not performed |
| Validation blind spot | Structure, presence, coverage pass while education fails |

**Sprint 44-2 response:** Eleven material-type contracts with minimum, strong, and failed realisation — Draft 1 accepted as reference for evaluation.
