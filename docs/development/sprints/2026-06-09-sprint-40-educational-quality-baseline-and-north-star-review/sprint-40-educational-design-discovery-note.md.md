\# Sprint 40 Educational Design Discovery Note



\## Purpose



Following Sprint 40 chartering, a parallel educational-design discovery activity was undertaken to explore the emerging educational philosophy underpinning PRISM.



This work sits outside the original Sprint 40 charter but is directly relevant to Workstream A (North Star Review) and subsequent Educational Quality Framework development.



The outputs of this work have been captured in:



```text

docs/educational-design/discovery/

└── educational-philosophy-and-quality-notes.md
```

\## Topics Explored



The discovery work examined a number of emerging educational principles including:



\### Learning Journeys



The learning journey emerged as the primary design unit.



A key conclusion was:



> PRISM optimises for coherent capability-building journeys rather than collections of individual activities.



\---



\### Capability Development



Learning was framed as the development of capability rather than the delivery of content.



Capability includes:



\* Understanding

\* Judgement

\* Decision-making

\* Creation

\* Critique

\* Application

\* Professional performance



\---



\### Constructivism



The work confirmed a constructivist position.



Learning is viewed as the construction of meaning rather than the transmission of information.



Instructional design therefore focuses on creating conditions for learning rather than simply presenting content.



\---



\### Adult–Adult Learning Relationships



Transactional Analysis emerged as a useful lens.



PRISM assumes learners are:



\* Capable

\* Agentic

\* Experienced

\* Able to exercise judgement



This has implications for:



\* Tone

\* Scaffolding

\* Feedback

\* Activity design

\* Learner autonomy



\---



\### Prior Knowledge and Explicit Assumptions



Learning journeys should:



\* Build on prior capability

\* Activate existing knowledge

\* Make assumptions explicit

\* Help learners self-position



The discussion concluded that educational quality depends less on perfectly targeting learners and more on clearly communicating assumptions and expectations.



\---



\### Scaffolding



Scaffolding was reframed as serving multiple functions:



\* Enabling success

\* Creating cognitive milieu

\* Directing cognition

\* Supporting increasing independence



\---



\### Evaluative Judgement



A major theme was the development of evaluative judgement.



PRISM should help learners:



\* Recognise quality

\* Compare alternatives

\* Critique reasoning

\* Calibrate their own thinking



\---



\### Feedback as Calibration



Higher-order learning often requires feedback that helps learners locate their thinking within a landscape of perspectives rather than simply identifying right and wrong answers.



The role of feedback evolves throughout a learning journey.



\---



\### Practice and Variation



A significant conclusion was that capability requires sufficient encounters with a class of problems.



Educational quality depends not merely on repetition but on meaningful variation that supports abstraction, transfer and independent performance.
