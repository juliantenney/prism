Activity: Inflation concepts warm-up  
Activity ID: A1  
Mapped outcomes: Activate prior knowledge, Agree working definition of inflation  

Material: M1 (scenario)  
Purpose: Price change scenarios  
Content:  
**Scenario 1: Household A**  
- Last year, the price of a loaf of bread was $2. This year, it costs $2.20.  
- The monthly electricity bill was $50 last year and is now $55.  
- The family’s income has remained the same over the year.  

**Scenario 2: Household B**  
- Last year, a movie ticket cost $10. This year, it costs $11.  
- The price of gasoline was $3 per litre last year and is now $3.30 per litre.  
- The household’s income increased by 5% this year.  

---

Material: M2 (task_cards)  
Purpose: Warm-up cards  
Content:  
**Task Card 1:** What do you think the word "inflation" means? Write your own definition in one sentence.  
**Task Card 2:** Look at the price changes in the scenarios. Which items have increased in price? List them.  
**Task Card 3:** How might inflation affect a household’s spending? Discuss and note your ideas.  
**Task Card 4:** Can inflation be good or bad? Give one reason for each.  
**Task Card 5:** Using the scenarios, explain if the families are experiencing inflation. Why or why not?  
**Task Card 6:** Agree on one shared definition of inflation for your group. Write it down clearly.

Activity: Measuring Inflation: Indicator Comparison  
Activity ID: A2  
Mapped outcomes: Compare inflation indicators, Understand measurement differences  

Material: M1 (comparison_table)  
Purpose: Indicator comparison  
Content:  
| Indicator     | What it measures                      | Main use                         | Example items included          | Frequency of update | Limitations                     |  
|---------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------|--------------------------------|---------------------|--------------------------------|  
| CPI (Consumer Price Index) | Average price change of goods and services bought by households | Measures cost of living changes for consumers | Food, clothing, housing, transport | Monthly             | May not reflect all spending patterns |  
| PPI (Producer Price Index) | Average price change received by producers for goods and services | Measures inflation at the wholesale or production level | Raw materials, intermediate goods | Monthly             | Does not reflect consumer prices directly |  
| GDP Deflator  | Price change of all goods and services produced domestically | Measures overall inflation in the economy | All domestically produced goods and services | Quarterly           | Includes investment and government spending, not just consumer prices |  

---

Material: M2 (prompt_set)  
Purpose: Discussion prompts  
Content:  
1. How do the CPI, PPI, and GDP deflator differ in what they measure?  
2. Why might policymakers prefer one indicator over the others?  
3. What are some challenges in using these indicators to understand inflation?

Activity: Household scenario — fixed income  
Activity ID: A3  
Mapped outcomes: Analyse inflation impact on fixed income households  

Material: M1 (scenario)  
Purpose: Household scenarios  
Content:  
**Scenario A:** Retired couple living on a fixed pension. Prices for food and utilities have increased by 10% this year. Their income is fixed and does not adjust with inflation.  
**Scenario B:** Single parent on a fixed salary contract. Rent and childcare costs have risen by 8%. Salary remains unchanged.  
**Scenario C:** Elderly individual receiving fixed social benefits. Medicine and healthcare prices have increased by 12%. Benefits are not inflation-indexed.  
**Scenario D:** Disabled person on fixed disability payments. Transportation and food costs have risen by 9%. Payments remain fixed.  

---

Material: M2 (analysis_table)  
Purpose: Worksheet table  
Content:  
| Scenario | Who is affected?                     | Main price pressure        | Policy angle                  |  
|----------|------------------------------------|----------------------------|------------------------------|  
| A        | Retired couple                     | Food, utilities            | Consider pension indexation  |  
| B        | Single parent                      | Rent, childcare            | Rent control, wage support   |  
| C        | Elderly individual                 | Medicine, healthcare       | Healthcare subsidies         |  
| D        | Disabled person                   | Transportation, food       | Disability payment adjustments|

Activity: Household scenario — variable costs  
Activity ID: A4  
Mapped outcomes: Compare inflation effects on variable cost households  

Material: M1 (scenario)  
Purpose: Cost scenarios  
Content:  
**Scenario 1:** Small business owner facing a 15% increase in raw material costs but stable sales revenue.  
**Scenario 2:** Freelancer experiencing a 10% rise in software subscription fees and a 5% increase in client payments.  
**Scenario 3:** Retail worker whose transportation costs have risen by 12%, but wages have not increased.

Material: M2 (impact_table)

Content:  
| Scenario  | Cost pressure         | Revenue pressure       | Short-run response                |

| 2         | Software fees +10%    | Client payments +5%   | Negotiate fees or cut expenses   |  
| 3         | Transport +12%        | Wages unchanged       | Reduce discretionary spending    |
