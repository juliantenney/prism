\# SPRINT-51-FINDING-01-LO1-TEACHING-MOVES.md



\## Context



Reviewed LO1-A1 (Mapping Class Relations and Value Flow) across:



\- Activity Design

\- Activity Materials

\- Page Artefact

\- Rendered Learner Experience



\## Finding



The activity does not support the hypothesis that pedagogic value is being significantly lost during compose or rendering.



Key pedagogic structures survive into the learner experience, including:



\- Orient

\- Think

\- Worked Example

\- Model Output

\- Checklist

\- Misconception Support



\## Interpretation



Sprint 51 should not assume that missing pedagogic value is primarily a preservation problem.



For this activity, the learner receives a coherent instructional experience.



The remaining gap is educational richness rather than pedagogic presence.



\## Missing Teaching Moves



The most significant opportunities are:



\### 1. Expert Judgement Visibility



Learners see good work but are not always shown:



\- why it is good

\- how experts recognise quality

\- why alternatives are weaker



\### 2. Comparative Judgement



Learners rarely see:



\- weak vs strong

\- descriptive vs analytical

\- novice vs expert



comparisons.



\### 3. Metacognitive Modelling



Learners are asked to check work but are not consistently shown:



\- how experts self-monitor

\- how experts identify weaknesses

\- how experts improve reasoning



\### 4. Significance Making



Learners understand task purpose but are not always shown:



\- why the concept matters

\- why the idea is important within the discipline

\- how it connects to wider understanding



\## Emerging Sprint 51 Hypothesis



The primary Sprint 51 challenge may not be:



"How do we preserve pedagogy?"



It may instead be:



"How do we make expert judgement, expert reasoning, and expert self-monitoring more visible within an already coherent instructional experience?"



\## Candidate Enhancement Patterns



1\. Why This Is Strong

2\. Weak → Better → Strong comparisons

3\. Expert Self-Check

4\. Why This Matters



These patterns should be evaluated against additional activities before being adopted more broadly.
