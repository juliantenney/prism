### Worked Example: Understanding Inflation and Its Causes

**Step 1: Define Inflation**  
Inflation is the sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services in an economy over a period of time. It means that, on average, prices rise, and the purchasing power of money decreases.

**Step 2: Identify the Main Causes of Inflation**  
There are two primary causes of inflation:  
- **Demand-pull inflation:** This occurs when aggregate demand in the economy exceeds aggregate supply, causing prices to rise. For example, increased consumer spending or government expenditure can push demand beyond supply capacity.  
- **Cost-push inflation:** This happens when the costs of production increase, such as higher wages or raw material prices, leading producers to raise prices to maintain profit margins.

**Step 3: Connect Causes to Economic Context**  
Inflation affects the economy by reducing the real value of money, which impacts consumers’ purchasing power and can influence interest rates and wage negotiations. For instance, if inflation is caused by demand-pull factors during economic growth, it may signal an overheating economy. Conversely, cost-push inflation might be linked to supply shocks like rising oil prices.

Content:  
### Sample Learner Explanation of Inflation and Its Causes

Inflation is when the overall prices of goods and services increase over time, which means money doesn’t buy as much as before. One main cause is demand-pull inflation, which happens when people want to buy more than what is available, pushing prices up. Another cause is cost-push inflation, where the costs for businesses, like wages or materials, rise and they increase prices to cover these costs. Inflation affects the economy by lowering money’s value and can lead to higher interest rates or changes in wages.

### Task Cards: Explaining Inflation and Its Causes

- **Card 1:** Read the worked example carefully. Identify the definition of inflation and write it in your own words.  
- **Card 2:** List the two main causes of inflation described. For each, write a brief explanation and an example.  
- **Card 3:** Explain how these causes relate to the wider economy. Why do demand-pull and cost-push factors lead to rising prices?  
- **Card 4:** Use the checklist below to verify your explanation:  
  - Have you defined inflation as a general increase in prices?  
  - Did you include at least two causes with clear reasoning?  
  - Did you connect causes to economic effects?  
  - Did you avoid confusing inflation with price changes of single items?

### Household Inflation Impact Scenarios

**Household A: The Smith Family**  
- Income: $50,000 per year (fixed salary)  
- Monthly Expenses: $3,000 (including $500 for groceries, $1,000 for rent, $300 for utilities, $1,200 for other expenses)  
- Inflation Rate: 5% annually  
- Context: The Smiths have a fixed income with limited ability to increase earnings. Prices for groceries and utilities have risen sharply.

**Household B: The Lee Family**  
- Income: $70,000 per year (includes variable bonuses)  
- Monthly Expenses: $4,000 (including $600 for groceries, $1,200 for rent, $400 for utilities, $1,800 for other expenses)  
- Inflation Rate: 5% annually  
- Context: The Lees have some flexibility in income due to bonuses and have recently negotiated a rent freeze.
