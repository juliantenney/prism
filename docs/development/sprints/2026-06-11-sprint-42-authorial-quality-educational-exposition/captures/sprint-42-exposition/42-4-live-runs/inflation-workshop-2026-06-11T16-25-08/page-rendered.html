First-Year Economics Workshop: Supply and Demand Fundamentals

Workshop Overview

This workshop introduces you to the fundamental economic concepts of supply and demand, how they determine market equilibrium, and how shifts in these curves affect prices and quantities. You will also develop skills to calculate equilibrium price and quantity using algebraic methods. These concepts are essential for understanding how markets operate and for analyzing real-world economic situations.

Learning Purpose

Mastering supply and demand principles equips you to explain market behavior, analyze changes in market conditions, and perform quantitative calculations to find equilibrium outcomes. By engaging with graphical and algebraic representations, you will build a strong foundation for further economic study and practical application.

Worked Example: Understanding Supply and Demand Curves and Market Equilibrium

This example illustrates how supply and demand curves interact to determine the market equilibrium price and quantity.

  • Supply Curve: Shows the relationship between price and quantity supplied. As price increases, suppliers are willing to offer more goods.
  • Demand Curve: Shows the relationship between price and quantity demanded. As price decreases, consumers want to buy more.

Graphical Illustration:

  • The supply curve slopes upward from left to right.
  • The demand curve slopes downward from left to right.
  • The point where these two curves intersect is the market equilibrium.

Stepwise Explanation: 1. At prices above equilibrium, quantity supplied exceeds quantity demanded, causing a surplus. Sellers lower prices to clear excess stock. 2. At prices below equilibrium, quantity demanded exceeds quantity supplied, causing a shortage. Buyers compete, pushing prices up. 3. The equilibrium price is where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied, and the market clears with no surplus or shortage. 4. Changes in supply or demand shift the curves, moving the equilibrium point accordingly.

This example helps you visualize how buyers and sellers interact in a market to set prices and quantities.

Modelling Note: Supply and Demand Curve Shifts and Market Effects

This note explains how shifts in supply and demand curves affect market price and quantity.

Shift in Demand Curve:
  • Increase in demand: The demand curve shifts rightward. At every price, consumers want more.
  • Effect: Equilibrium price and quantity both increase.
  • Decrease in demand: The demand curve shifts leftward. Consumers want less at every price.
  • Effect: Equilibrium price and quantity both decrease.
Shift in Supply Curve:
  • Increase in supply: The supply curve shifts rightward. Suppliers offer more at every price.
  • Effect: Equilibrium price decreases, quantity increases.
  • Decrease in supply: The supply curve shifts leftward. Suppliers offer less at every price.
  • Effect: Equilibrium price increases, quantity decreases.

Important Distinction:

  • Shift of a curve means the entire curve moves left or right due to external factors (e.g., income changes, production costs).
  • Movement along a curve means a change in quantity demanded or supplied due to a price change, not a shift.

Understanding these differences helps correctly analyze market changes.

Output

A completed analysis table correctly categorizing shifts, causes, and effects, demonstrating ability to interpret graphical market changes.

Support note: Focus on distinguishing shifts from movements along curves and carefully link causes to effects in your explanations.

Calculating Market Equilibrium

Duration: 20 mins

Quantitative skills are essential for applying economic concepts. This activity develops your ability to calculate equilibrium price and quantity using supply and demand equations, a key tool for market analysis.

How to think: Follow stepwise algebraic reasoning to solve for equilibrium by setting supply equal to demand and solving for price and quantity.

What to do

  • Study the worked example solving equilibrium from given linear supply and demand equations. Then, apply the method to calculate equilibrium price and quantity for provided practice problems.
Reflect

Explain each step of your calculation process and why setting supply equal to demand finds the equilibrium.

Task cards

Card 1

Task Cards: Calculating Equilibrium Price and Quantity

  1. Given demand \( Q_d = 150 - 3P \) and supply \( Q_s = 30 + 2P \), calculate the equilibrium price and quantity. Show all steps.
  2. For demand \( Q_d = 200 - 4P \) and supply \( Q_s = 50 + P \), find the equilibrium price and quantity. Explain your reasoning.
  3. Given demand \( Q_d = 120 - P \) and supply \( Q_s = 40 + 4P \), calculate equilibrium and describe what the result means.

Write your stepwise calculations and explanations clearly.

Checklist

  • ☐Did you correctly set the demand and supply equations equal to each other?
  • ☐Did you solve the resulting equation accurately for price?
  • ☐Did you substitute the equilibrium price back into one of the equations to find quantity?
  • ☐Did you explain why setting supply equal to demand finds the equilibrium?
  • ☐Are your calculations clearly shown step-by-step?
  • ☐If you missed any step, revisit the worked example and redo the calculations.
Worked Example: Calculating Market Equilibrium from Equations

Given the supply and demand equations:

  • Demand: \( Q_d = 100
  • 2P \)
  • Supply: \( Q_s = 20 + 3P \)

Step 1: Set quantity demanded equal to quantity supplied to find equilibrium: \[ 100 - 2P = 20 + 3P \]

Step 2: Solve for \(P\): \[ 100 - 20 = 3P + 2P \\ 80 = 5P \\ P = \frac{80}{5} = 16 \]

Step 3: Substitute \(P=16\) into either equation to find equilibrium quantity: \[ Q_d = 100 - 2(16) = 100 - 32 = 68 \]

Step 4: Interpretation:

  • The equilibrium price is 16.
  • The equilibrium quantity is 68 units.

Why this works: Equilibrium occurs where the quantity consumers want to buy equals the quantity producers want to sell. Setting the equations equal finds that price and quantity.

Output

Written calculations showing stepwise solution of equilibrium price and quantity for each practice problem, with explanations.

Support note: Ensure you understand the rationale for equating supply and demand functions and carefully check your algebraic steps.
