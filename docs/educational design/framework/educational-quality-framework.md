# PRISM Educational Quality Framework

## Purpose

This framework defines educational quality within PRISM. It provides a domain-independent model for designing, generating and evaluating learning experiences.

The framework is intended to be:

* Educationally coherent
* Practical for learning designers
* Operationalisable within PRISM
* Independent of any specific technology or platform

---

# North Star

PRISM designs coherent learner journeys that enable meaningful learning success through the development of understanding, capability, judgement and independence.

---

# Learner Transformations

Educational quality is defined by positive learner transformation.

## Understanding

Learners develop increasingly accurate, connected and transferable understanding.

Characteristics include:

* Conceptual understanding
* Pattern recognition
* Relationship building
* Explanation and articulation
* Refinement of mental models

---

## Capability

Learners develop the ability to apply knowledge effectively in practice.

Characteristics include:

* Application
* Practice
* Adaptation
* Problem solving
* Transfer to new situations

---

## Judgement

Learners develop the ability to evaluate quality, make informed decisions and critique alternatives.

Characteristics include:

* Comparison
* Evaluation
* Justification
* Calibration
* Decision making

---

## Independence

Learners develop increasing capacity for self-direction, self-regulation and autonomous learning.

Characteristics include:

* Self-assessment
* Planning
* Monitoring
* Reflection
* Independent application

---

# Design Principles

## Journey Coherence

Learning experiences should form a coherent developmental journey rather than a collection of disconnected activities.

---

## Active Construction

Learners actively construct, test, articulate and refine understanding.

---

## Authenticity

Learning should connect to meaningful contexts, realistic problems and genuine forms of practice.

---

## Judgement Development

Learning should deliberately develop evaluative judgement rather than focusing solely on knowledge acquisition and task completion.

---

## Progressive Independence

Responsibility should progressively transfer from the resource, facilitator or teacher to the learner.

# Cross-Cutting Process

## Metacognition

Metacognition supports all learner transformations.

Manifestations include:

* Reflection
* Confidence monitoring
* Uncertainty recognition
* Planning
* Self-assessment
* Progress awareness

Metacognition functions as the connective process linking understanding, capability, judgement and independence.

---

# Learning Success

Learning experiences should enable learners to recognise successful development.

Indicators may include:

* Increased confidence
* Increased competence
* Improved judgement
* Increased learner agency
* Sustained engagement
* Visible progress

Learning success is not a separate educational outcome but an indicator that meaningful development is occurring.

---

# Realisation Layer

The framework is realised through learning activities, resources and experiences.

Examples include:

* Explain
* Explore
* Analyse
* Compare
* Apply
* Create
* Evaluate
* Reflect
* Investigate
* Discuss

These activity types are not educational principles. They are manifestations of educational intent.

---

# Core Design Rule

PRISM optimises for cognitive activity rather than interface activity.

Learning quality is determined by the thinking undertaken by the learner, not by the quantity of software interaction.

Interactivity should be used only when it contributes meaningfully to understanding, capability, judgement or independence.
