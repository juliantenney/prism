\# PRISM Educational Prompting Guide



\## Purpose



This document explains how the PRISM Educational Quality Framework is translated into generation instructions.



The Educational Quality Framework defines what educational quality means.



This guide defines how those principles should be expressed during generation.



\---



\# Design Principle



Generation should prioritise learner development over content coverage.



Resources should be designed as coherent learner journeys that progressively develop:



\* Understanding

\* Capability

\* Judgement

\* Independence



\---



\# Prompting for Learner Transformations



\## Understanding



\### Intent



Develop increasingly accurate, connected and transferable understanding.



\### Prompt Language



Use language such as:



\* Explain

\* Connect

\* Compare

\* Articulate

\* Refine

\* Relate

\* Identify patterns

\* Build understanding



\### Expected Manifestations



Learners:



\* Build mental models

\* Connect concepts

\* Explain relationships

\* Refine understanding



\---



\## Capability



\### Intent



Develop the ability to apply knowledge effectively.



\### Prompt Language



Use language such as:



\* Practise

\* Apply

\* Adapt

\* Solve

\* Use

\* Demonstrate

\* Transfer



\### Expected Manifestations



Learners:



\* Apply knowledge

\* Perform tasks

\* Solve problems

\* Transfer learning to new contexts



\---



\## Judgement



\### Intent



Develop evaluative judgement and decision-making.



\### Prompt Language



Use language such as:



\* Compare

\* Evaluate

\* Critique

\* Justify

\* Rank

\* Defend

\* Assess quality



\### Expected Manifestations



Learners:



\* Compare alternatives

\* Evaluate quality

\* Justify decisions

\* Defend conclusions



\---



\## Independence



\### Intent



Develop learner ownership and self-direction.



\### Prompt Language



Use language such as:



\* Reflect

\* Self-assess

\* Plan

\* Monitor

\* Decide

\* Direct your learning



\### Expected Manifestations



Learners:



\* Make decisions independently

\* Monitor progress

\* Plan future action

\* Take ownership of learning



\---



\# Prompting for Design Principles



\## Journey Coherence



Prompt for:



\* A coherent learner journey

\* Developmental progression

\* Connected learning activities



Avoid:



\* Collections of disconnected tasks

\* Topic lists

\* Content-first structures



\---



\## Active Construction



Prompt for:



\* Thinking

\* Reasoning

\* Explanation

\* Knowledge construction



Avoid:



\* Passive content consumption

\* Excessive exposition



\---



\## Authenticity



Prompt for:



\* Realistic scenarios

\* Genuine problems

\* Meaningful examples

\* Real-world application



Avoid:



\* Artificial exercises

\* Context-free practice



\---



\## Judgement Development



Prompt for:



\* Comparison

\* Evaluation

\* Ranking

\* Critique

\* Justification



Avoid:



\* Recall-only activities

\* Binary right/wrong thinking



\---



\## Progressive Independence



Prompt for:



\* Gradual reduction of support

\* Increasing learner responsibility

\* Independent decision making



Typical progression:



Observe

→ Guided Practice

→ Supported Decision Making

→ Independent Decision Making

→ Transfer



\---



\# Prompting for Metacognition



Metacognition should be embedded throughout the learner journey.



Prompt for:



\* Reflection prompts

\* Confidence checks

\* Progress checks

\* Thinking prompts

\* Planning prompts



Metacognitive support should remain lightweight and supportive.



It should guide learner thinking rather than compete with the learning content.



\---



\# Prompting for Learning Success



Make learner progress visible.



Prompt learners to recognise:



\* What they understand now that they did not understand before

\* What they can now do that they could not do before

\* How their judgement is improving

\* Where they are becoming more independent



Learning success should be recognised throughout the journey rather than only at completion.



\---



\# Activity Guidance



Activity types are manifestations of educational intent.



Common activity types include:



\* Explain

\* Explore

\* Analyse

\* Compare

\* Apply

\* Create

\* Evaluate

\* Reflect

\* Investigate

\* Discuss



Activities should require learners to:



\* Think

\* Reason

\* Compare

\* Evaluate

\* Decide

\* Justify



Activities should be selected because they support learner transformation, not because they increase visible activity.



\---



\# Cognitive Activity over Interface Activity



PRISM prioritises cognitive activity over interface activity.



The primary educational value lies in:



\* Thinking

\* Comparing

\* Evaluating

\* Reflecting

\* Deciding

\* Justifying



Interactivity should only be used where it meaningfully contributes to:



\* Understanding

\* Capability

\* Judgement

\* Independence



Avoid interaction for its own sake.



A learner thinking deeply with a page and a pencil is often engaging in richer learning than a learner performing low-value interface interactions.
