## Intent

Close the session by **scaffolding learner-produced synthesis** — multiple closure angles, explicit production expectation, and structural support — **without supplying the completed consolidation answer** or collapsing prior exposition, judgement, or capstone work into a teacher-written summary.

---

## Instructional Problem

Sprint 43 identified **closure collapse** as a risk: closure materials that substitute for learner synthesis, pre-write capstone outputs, or replay outcomes lists instead of supporting reflection. Sprint 44 evaluation tested whether benchmark consolidation bodies perform the §5.9 instructional move.

Evidence:

- **Marx M17** — **Strong** in Pass 2 and Inter-Rater Validation (unanimous). Learner must write 80–120 words; three closure angles; sentence starters; no pre-written synthesis; distinction-making prompt (explanation vs prediction).
- **Photosynthesis M23** — **verdict split**: Pass 2 **Minimum**; Inter-Rater **Strong**. Both agree M23 scaffolds learner writing with multiple prompts and an 80-word band; disagree on whether bullet prompts alone meet strong closure depth.
- **Neither body** exhibited §5.9 failure modes of model essay, past-tense `"In this session you learned…"` summary, or pre-written capstone fulfilment — Pass 2 explicitly noted this for M17 and M23.

The instructional problem SP-04 addresses is **FM-10** (thin judgement/closure scaffold): closure that meets minimum slot presence but lacks sentence starters, explicit recall/change/application framing, or criteria-linked self-check — Pass 2 applied this to M23; Inter-Rater did not withhold Strong on M23.

SP-04 documents **what closure characteristics evaluation treated as strong on M17** and **where M23 divided reviewers**, without inventing closure requirements beyond corpus findings.

---

## Minimum Realisation Shape

Minimum structure evidenced on **both** M17 and M23 (Pass 2 scored M23 Minimum, not Failed; Inter-Rater scored both at least Minimum):

1. **Distinct consolidation material** — typed `consolidation_summary` with dedicated heading (`## Consolidation Summary`); not relabelled prompt_set only.
2. **Learner-produced closure required** — imperative to write:
   - M17: `Write at least 80–120 words addressing:`.
   - M23: `Use these prompts to write your final summary (minimum 80 words)`.
3. **Multiple closure prompts** — at least three angles:
   - M17: three bullet prompts (what Marx explains effectively; limitations; key distinction).
   - M23: four bullet prompts (energy transformation; matter transformation; global importance; misconception to reject).
4. **No completed learner deliverable in the body** — neither material supplies the learner's consolidation essay or capstone judgement text. Pass 2: no capstone spoiler on M17; M23 does not pre-write synthesis.
5. **Integrative closing instruction** — M17: `Aim to summarise your understanding, not introduce new ideas`; M23: `Bring these together into a short explanation of why photosynthesis is one of the most important biological processes on Earth`.
6. **Word-band guidance** — M17: 80–120 words; M23: minimum 80 words (DLA: ≥80-word summary).

**Minimum does not require** (evidenced as absent on M23 when Pass 2 withheld Strong):

- Sentence starters (`You might begin with…`) — present on M17 only.
- Explicit recall / change / application angle labels — M17 uses effectiveness, limitation, distinction; M23 uses topic bullets; Pass 2 did not treat M23 bullets as explicit recall/change/application framing.
- Self-check questions tied to session criteria — not evidenced on either body.

## Strong Realisation Shape

### Evidenced on Marx M17 — unanimous Strong (Pass 2, Inter-Rater)

1. **Three+ distinct closure angles with evaluative/metacognitive depth:**
   - What Marx explains **most effectively**.
   - Where theory appears **limited or inaccurate**.
   - What **key distinction** the learner can now make (e.g. explanation vs prediction).
2. **Sentence starters that structure without filling:**
   - `You might begin with:` plus three starter lines (`One idea that now seems clearer is…`; `A limitation I can now explain is…`; `When evaluating theories, I now focus on…`).
3. **Operational word band** — `80–120 words`.
4. **Learner-write guardrail** — `Aim to summarise your understanding, not introduce new ideas` (scaffold only; blocks new-topic closure collapse).
5. **No past-tense declarative session summary** — prompts are forward/task-oriented, not `In this session you learned…`.
6. **No pre-written synthesis** — body contains prompts and starters only.

Pass 2: "Three+ closure angles; learner-write required; sentence starters without filling; no past-tense summary; no capstone spoiler. Strong consolidation scaffold."

Inter-Rater: aligned with Strong on same features.

### Evidenced on Photosynthesis M23 — disputed Strong

**Features present (both passes acknowledged):**

7. **Four closure-angle bullets** covering session themes (energy transformation, matter transformation, global significance, misconception rejection).
8. **Minimum word band** — `minimum 80 words`.
9. **Integrative synthesis instruction** — bring prompts together into one explanation of global importance.
10. **No pre-written learner summary** in body.

**Pass 2 withheld Strong because:**

- "Prompt bullets only — no sentence starters, no self-check tied to session criteria, no recall/change/application framing as explicit angles (vs Marx M17)."
- Classified as **Minimum**; **FM-10** (thin closure scaffold).

**Inter-Rater awarded Strong:**

- Four angles; learner-write with word band; scaffold without completed answer; integrative close.

### Not evidenced as required for Strong in this pattern entry

- Self-check questions tied to session criteria — absent on M17 and M23; not cited as blocking M17 Strong.
- Transfer cue in consolidation body — M16 (Marx) and M22 (Photosynthesis) are separate transfer materials; not inside M17/M23 bodies.
- Criteria-linked self-check — not on either consolidation body.

### Suggested strong shape (M17 unanimous + §5.9 contract; M23 partial)

Combine M17 features (sentence starters, distinction-making, evaluative angles, word band, learner-write guardrail) with multi-angle prompts evidenced on both bodies. M23 demonstrates that **multi-bullet scaffold + word band + integrative instruction** may suffice for Strong (Inter-Rater) or remain Minimum / FM-10 (Pass 2).

## Detection Signals

### Minimum

Reviewer can confirm (M17 and M23 — both passes):

- [ ] Distinct `consolidation_summary` material with heading.
- [ ] Learner instructed to write (not read a supplied summary).
- [ ] ≥3 closure prompts or angles.
- [ ] Word-band or length expectation stated.
- [ ] No full expected_output / capstone text pre-written in body.
- [ ] **FM-10 risk (Pass 2)** if bullet prompts only, no sentence starters, no explicit recall/change/distinction framing — Pass 2 applied to M23.

### Strong

Reviewer can confirm — **M17 unanimous precedent**:

- [ ] Sentence starters or comparison frames that structure without filling.
- [ ] Evaluative or metacognitive angles (effectiveness, limitation, distinction-making).
- [ ] Word band stated.
- [ ] Learner-write guardrail (summarise understanding; do not introduce new ideas).
- [ ] Reviewer can distinguish scaffold from teacher-completed synthesis.

**M23:** declare Pass 2 (Minimum / FM-10) vs Inter-Rater (Strong) stance.

### Weak

Below strong; may still be Minimum:

- Bullet prompts without starters (M23 — Pass 2).
- Angles named by topic only without evaluative/metacognitive framing (M23 energy/matter/global/misconception vs M17 effectiveness/limitation/distinction).
- Integrative line present but thin structural support.

### Failed

Not evidenced on M17 or M23. §5.9 Failed would apply to model essay closure, past-tense summary substituting for learner reflection, or prompt_set only — **not observed** on these materials. Photosynthesis capstone `page.json` truncation of M23 (Pass 2 composition note) is channel failure (FM-12), not body Failed.

## Authoring Guidance

Evidence-backed guidance only.

1. **Require learner-written consolidation** — both exemplars use write/summarise imperatives; do not supply completed closure prose.

2. **Provide ≥3 closure angles** — M17 (three prompts); M23 (four bullets). DLA M17 specifies insights across strengths, limitations, evaluation approach.

3. **State a word band** — M17 `80–120 words`; M23 `minimum 80 words`.

4. **Add sentence starters that structure without filling** — evidenced on unanimous Strong M17; absent on disputed M23.

5. **Include evaluative or metacognitive angles where session is evaluate-shaped** — M17 effectiveness, limitation, distinction (explanation vs prediction). M23 includes misconception rejection as one angle.

6. **Close with integrative instruction** — M23 `Bring these together into…`; M17 `summarise your understanding, not introduce new ideas`.

7. **Do not pre-write the learner's consolidation or capstone** — neither benchmark violated; addresses closure-collapse risk from Sprint 43.

8. **Avoid past-tense declarative teacher summaries** — `In this session you learned…` not evidenced; §5.9 failure mode.

9. **Do not collapse judgement, rubric, or exposition into closure** — keep consolidation distinct; capstone judgement remains in template/table/transfer materials (Marx A4 arc).

10. **Bullet-only prompts may score Minimum (Pass 2)** even with four angles and word band — authors targeting Strong without calibration risk should use M17 scaffold depth.

## Reviewer Guidance

Evaluate a `consolidation_summary` body against 44-2 §5.9:

1. **Failed check** — Pre-written synthesis, model essay, past-tense teacher summary, or prompt_set only (not evidenced on M17/M23; apply per contract if observed elsewhere).
2. **Minimum check** — Distinct material; learner-write required; ≥3 angles; word band; no completed deliverable in body (M17, M23 both pass).
3. **FM-10 check** — Thin scaffold: bullets only, no starters, no evaluative/metacognitive framing. Pass 2 applied to M23; Inter-Rater did not on M23.
4. **Strong check** — M17 signals: sentence starters, distinction-making, evaluative angles, learner-write guardrail. For M23-like bodies, **declare Pass 2 vs Inter-Rater stance**.
5. **Closure-collapse check** — Confirm body scaffolds rather than completes learner synthesis (both benchmark bodies pass).
6. **Composition** — Record page truncation separately (FM-12).
