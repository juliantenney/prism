Activity: Evaluating Project Proposals  
Activity ID: A3  
Mapped outcomes: LO3, LO4  

Material: M1 (criteria_exposition)  
Purpose: To explain the evaluation criteria for project proposals  
Content:  
When evaluating project proposals, it is essential to use clear, operational criteria that reflect the goals and constraints of the project. The following three dimensions will guide your assessment:  

1. **Feasibility**: Assess whether the project can realistically be completed within the given resources, time, and technical constraints. Consider the availability of skills, technology, and budget.  
2. **Impact**: Evaluate the potential benefits or value the project will deliver to stakeholders or the community. This includes social, economic, environmental, or organizational impact.  
3. **Innovation**: Consider how novel or creative the project approach is compared to existing solutions. Innovative projects may offer unique advantages or address problems in new ways.  

Each proposal should be rated on these criteria to ensure a balanced and thorough evaluation.  

Material: M2 (scenario)  
Purpose: To provide case narratives illustrating different project proposals  
Content:  
**Scenario 1: Community Garden Initiative**  
The local council has received a proposal to create a community garden in an urban neighborhood. The project aims to improve green space, promote healthy eating, and foster community engagement. The proposal outlines a budget of $20,000, a timeline of six months, and partnerships with local schools and volunteers.  

**Scenario 2: Mobile Health Clinic**  
A nonprofit organization proposes a mobile health clinic to serve rural areas lacking medical facilities. The project requests $50,000 in funding, plans to operate over one year, and includes hiring medical staff and purchasing a vehicle equipped for basic health services. The proposal emphasizes improving access to healthcare and reducing travel burdens for patients.  

Material: M3 (worked_example)  
Purpose: To model the evaluation of a project proposal using the criteria  
Content:  
Let’s evaluate the Community Garden Initiative proposal using the criteria:  

- **Feasibility**: The budget of $20,000 and six-month timeline appear reasonable given the scope and partnerships. Local schools and volunteers provide additional support, increasing feasibility. Rating: Strong.  
- **Impact**: The project targets urban green space improvement and community health, which are valuable outcomes. However, the scale is limited to one neighborhood. Rating: Moderate.  
- **Innovation**: Community gardens are common initiatives; the proposal does not introduce novel methods or technology. Rating: Weak.  

**Summary**: The proposal is feasible and impactful locally but lacks innovation. This balanced evaluation helps decision-makers prioritize projects that meet multiple goals.

Material: M5 (independent_judgement_template)  
Purpose: To scaffold learner independent evaluation and reporting of a new project proposal  
Content:  

**Project Proposal Evaluation Memo**  

**1. Proposal Overview**  
Briefly describe the project proposal you are evaluating.  

**2. Evaluation Criteria Ratings**  
- Feasibility (1-5): _____  
- Impact (1-5): _____  
- Innovation (1-5): _____  

**3. Justifications**  
- Feasibility: Explain your rating with reference to resources, timeline, and risks.  
- Impact: Explain your rating with reference to benefits and stakeholders.  
- Innovation: Explain your rating with reference to novelty and creativity.  

**4. Overall Recommendation**  
Based on your evaluation, would you recommend funding this project? Why or why not?  

*Use clear, concise language and support your judgements with evidence from the proposal.*  

Material: M6 (verification_checklist)  
Purpose: To confirm understanding and quality of evaluation judgments  
Content:  

**Evaluation Quality Checklist**  

- [ ] Have I applied all three criteria (Feasibility, Impact, Innovation) in my evaluation?  
- [ ] Are my ratings supported by clear, specific justifications?  
- [ ] Did I consider both strengths and weaknesses of the proposal?  
- [ ] Is my overall recommendation consistent with my ratings and justifications?  
- [ ] Have I avoided personal bias and based my judgement on evidence?  

If you answer “No” to any of these, revisit your evaluation to improve clarity and reasoning.  

Material: M7 (transfer_prompt)  
Purpose: To encourage application of evaluation skills in learners’ own contexts  
Content:  

Think about a project or initiative in your own community, workplace, or area of interest that requires evaluation.  

- How would you apply the Feasibility, Impact, and Innovation criteria to assess this project?  
- What specific factors would you consider under each criterion in your context?  
- How might your evaluation influence decision-making or resource allocation?  

Write a brief plan (at least 80 words) describing how you would conduct this evaluation, highlighting any adaptations you would make to the criteria or process.
