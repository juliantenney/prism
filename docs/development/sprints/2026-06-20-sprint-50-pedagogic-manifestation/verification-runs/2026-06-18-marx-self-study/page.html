Understanding Inflation: Causes, Effects, and Analysis

Session

55 min session

Introduction and Learning Purpose

This session explores the concept of inflation, focusing on its causes and effects within an economy. You will develop your understanding of different inflationary mechanisms, learn to classify inflation scenarios, and analyze their impacts. By engaging with real-world examples and structured activities, you will enhance your ability to evaluate inflation causes critically and independently.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the main causes of inflation including demand-pull and cost-push factors.
  • Classify inflation scenarios based on their underlying mechanisms.
  • Analyze the impact of inflation on different economic agents.
  • Develop reasoned judgments about inflation causes using evidence.
  • Apply economic concepts to real-world inflation cases.

Formative Assessment Check

1

Question 1

Which of the following best describes demand-pull inflation?

  • A. A sudden increase in production costs.
  • B. An increase in consumer demand leading to higher prices.
  • C. A wage-price spiral causing rising wages and prices.
  • D. A decrease in aggregate demand.
2

Question 2

What is a typical effect of cost-push inflation on producers?

  • A. Increased profits due to higher sales volume.
  • B. Reduced costs leading to higher output.
  • C. Higher production costs that may reduce profits.
  • D. No impact on production costs.
3

Question 3

In a wage-price spiral, what is the relationship between wages and prices?

  • A. Wages rise first, causing prices to fall.
  • B. Prices rise first, causing wages to fall.
  • C. Wages and prices rise in a reinforcing cycle.
  • D. Wages and prices remain stable.
