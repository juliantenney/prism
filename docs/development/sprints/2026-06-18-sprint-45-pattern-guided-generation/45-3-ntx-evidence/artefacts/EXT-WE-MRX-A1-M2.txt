Worked example: analysing a workplace scenario with Marxist concepts

Scenario:
A delivery company introduces route-tracking software that increases the number of deliveries each worker completes per shift, but wages remain unchanged.

Step 1 - Identify class position
Workers sell labor for wages and do not own the delivery platform or logistics infrastructure. The company owners control production resources and strategic decisions.

Step 2 - Trace value creation and surplus
Because each worker now completes more deliveries per shift, total value created per worker rises. If wages stay the same, the gap between produced value and paid wage increases.

Step 3 - Interpret exploitation in this case
In Marxist terms, exploitation appears in the enlarged surplus retained by owners. Productivity gains are captured as profit unless redistributed through higher pay or reduced labor intensity.

Step 4 - Connect to broader dynamics
This process can intensify class tension: workers may demand compensation for higher output, while owners may prioritize accumulation and competitive advantage.

Conclusion:
The scenario shows how technological efficiency can increase surplus extraction under capitalism, reinforcing class conflict around wages, control, and distribution.
